Black wolf

A black wolf is a melanistic colour variant of the gray wolf (Canis lupus). [1][2] Black specimens are recorded among red wolves (Canis rufus), and these colour variants are probably still around today.[3] Genetic research from the Stanford University School of Medicine and the University of California, Los Angeles revealed that wolves with black pelts owe their distinctive coloration to a mutation which occurred in domestic dogs, and was carried to wolves through wolf-dog hybridization
